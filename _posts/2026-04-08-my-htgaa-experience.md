---
I’ve heard about synthetic biology for quite a while, especially through my involvement with SynBio Indonesia—but I never really had the chance to study it in depth. Back in 2023, I first came across *How to Grow (Almost) Anything*, a course by MIT Media Lab and Harvard, through a group chat. They were offering free access for anyone to join as a listener, which sounded amazing. I tried to follow it that year, but I was in the middle of my final project and just couldn’t keep up.

So in 2025, I decided to give it another shot, this time as a committed listener. There are actually two types of listeners: global (you just join and listen whenever you can) and committed (you attend everything, do the homework, and fully participate). I chose the second one… which, in hindsight, was both a great and slightly crazy decision.

The course itself is intense. Every week there’s around 3 hours of lectures, plus 1-2 hours of recitation, and assignments that are honestly quite challenging. They also introduced global nodes to support learners locally, and I joined the BioClub Tokyo node since the timezone worked a bit better for me. Still, most lectures happened around 2 AM Jakarta time—so yes, sometimes I just logged into Zoom, went back to sleep, and watched the recording later (not my proudest habit, but we survive 😅).

That said, this is probably the hardest course I’ve ever taken, and also one of the most inspiring. The lectures are delivered by real experts from around the world, and the learning environment feels very different. The students come from all kinds of backgrounds, not just biology, but also engineering, architecture, design, even public policy. It made the discussions super interesting and opened my perspective on how interdisciplinary synthetic biology really is.

The assignments were tough, and the final project was even tougher. I remember feeling completely stuck at first, not knowing what idea to pursue. In the end, I worked on a project about engineering yeast to produce drugs, which, honestly, still feels a bit surreal to say. The final presentations were wild too, they went on for more than 9 hours! Huge respect to people like David (Kong) who stayed for the entire session.

Overall, I’d really recommend this course to anyone interested in synthetic biology. Not just for the content, but for the experience of being in a world-class university. You get to see how top institutions like MIT and Harvard teach, how students ask questions, and how ideas are explored in such an open, interdisciplinary way. It’s challenging, yes, but also incredibly rewarding.

If you’re curious, you can check out more about the course on their official website. I also built a personal website (a requirement for committed listeners) where I documented my assignments and final project, feel free to explore!
